Mind Islands redesign review board

Sea otter as the only self-externalized avatar.

These concept boards turn the otter into the primary emotional entry point, make conversational logging the default behavior, and push the islands into a more intentional secondary surface. Every option keeps the otter in first-person self-talk and avoids the “digital pet” feeling.

How to review this board

  • Pick the home-screen feeling you want first, before we touch production code.
  • Treat the avatar version and the flow version as a pair, not separate decorations.
  • Ask: does this feel like self-support, not a companion chatbot and not a game pet?
Fixed product rule The product has one avatar only: a sea otter. No more `owl`, `puppy`, or `girl`.
Primary home action “Tell me what happened today” becomes the main entry, powered by the log assistant.
Secondary world Islands still matter, but they become a world you enter intentionally rather than the first screen.
Interaction metaphor Everyday logs become shells. Extra-precious memories can later become stones kept close.
Concept 01

Lagoon Confidant

The calmest version. The home screen is almost entirely the otter and the conversation. Islands exist behind a gentle sheet, so the first emotional move is always speaking to yourself.

Best fit Most aligned with “record first, reflect softly, no dashboard energy.”

Avatar variant A: Velvet Otter

  • Silhouette Rounded 3/4 pose, tucked paws, heartline chest pocket.
  • Material Velvet fur gradients, warm belly glow, low-contrast rim light.
  • Motion Breathing drift, tiny head tilt, shell reveal from chest pocket.
  • Atmosphere Smoky plum lagoon with mossy reflections, not visible ocean blue.

Flow summary

  • Home = otter + bubble + log input + one islands button.
  • After confirm, a shell glides into the Memories sheet.
  • Manual island editing stays available, but one level deeper.

Main risk

The islands world may feel too hidden unless we give the secondary entry enough poetry and presence.

Concept 02

Pocket Ritual

A more tactile, object-centered interpretation. The otter’s pocket becomes a ritual space: shells are reviewed, held, and confirmed before being tucked into the larger island world.

Best fit Strongest shell/pocket metaphor, strongest sense of “precious memory” without making the product childish.

Avatar variant B: Riverstone Otter

  • Silhouette More upright torso, clearer chest pocket, paws closer to the body.
  • Material Smooth riverstone shading, shell-like highlights, slightly more sculptural.
  • Motion Pocket-opening gesture, shell weighing gesture, slower nods.
  • Atmosphere Wet stone, dusk plum, brushed copper, muted reeds.

Flow summary

  • Home includes otter, chat, and a visible “Pocket” tray with 1-3 recent shells.
  • Confirming a draft feels like placing something inside a cherished inner space.
  • Islands still live one step away, but the object ritual is the emotional anchor.

Main risk

If we over-materialize the pocket interaction, it can drift too close to “collectibles” and away from self-reflection.

Concept 03

Memory Courier

The most animated version. The home screen still starts with the otter, but the islands remain faintly present, and after confirmation the otter physically travels to place the shell on the right island.

Best fit Strongest “Mind Islands” world presence and strongest motion storytelling.

Avatar variant C: Tideglass Otter

  • Silhouette Longer body and clearer swim line, easier to animate between nodes.
  • Material Semi-luminous glassy highlights over soft fur, more 2.5D than plush.
  • Motion Swim arc, shell carry pose, return-to-rest pose.
  • Atmosphere Tidal dusk with island shadows already visible in the distance.

Flow summary

  • The world is visible but quiet on the first screen.
  • Confirming a shell triggers a short otter travel sequence.
  • This version should come after we validate the simpler conversation-first home.

Main risk

This is the easiest version to accidentally turn into a pet simulation. The wording and motion restraint have to stay extremely disciplined.

Cross-concept recommendation

If we want the strongest first implementation path, start with Lagoon Confidant. It aligns most closely with your core product intention and requires the least risky structural change. If you want the shell/pocket metaphor to become more materially expressive, borrow selected details from Pocket Ritual. Save Memory Courier for a second phase, once the conversation-first home is stable.
