# Mind Islands Market Research Slide Outline

Audience: pitch day judges, early angel investors, design or product-savvy evaluators

Recommended number of slides for the market section: 5

## Slide 1

### Title
The problem is large, visible, and underserved

### Body copy

- College students are facing sustained emotional pressure, not just occasional stress.
- In the 2023-2024 Healthy Minds Study, 38% screened positive for depression and 34% for anxiety.
- Yet support access is still constrained by time, cost, uncertainty, and stigma.

### Footer
Source: Healthy Minds Network, 2023-2024 National Data Report

## Slide 2

### Title
Young people already seek digital support, but current apps are only partially effective

### Body copy

- 54% of people age 14-22 have used an app to support their mental health or well-being.
- Most-used categories: sleep, mindfulness, happiness, stress reduction, and mood tracking.
- But only 16% say those apps were very helpful.

### Visual
Use: `assets/youth-app-category-demand.svg`

### Footer
Source: Hopelab x Common Sense Media, 2024

## Slide 3

### Title
The market is active, but fragmented

### Body copy

- Headspace and Calm own meditation and sleep.
- Daylio owns quick mood tracking.
- Finch proves that playful self-care can retain users.
- Wysa validates always-on AI support.
- No clear winner combines emotionally safe design, life-domain reflection, and game-like progression for stressed young adults.

### Speaker note
This is where you position `Mind Islands` as a category bridge, not just another wellness app.

## Slide 4

### Title
Mind Islands occupies a clear product white space

### Body copy

- Not a productivity dashboard
- Not a clinical therapy substitute
- Not just a mood tracker
- Not just an AI chatbot

`Mind Islands` turns daily pressure into structured self-observation through an avatar world, six life islands, and gentle AI-supported reflection.

### Speaker note
If challenged on competitors, say the closest emotional reference is Finch, but the core wedge is different: chronic pressure in student and early-career life, organized into meaningful life domains.

## Slide 5

### Title
The category is large and growing fast

### Body copy

- Global mental health apps market: $9.94B in 2025
- Projected to reach $22.73B by 2030
- 18.0% CAGR
- North America is already the largest region

Use: `assets/mental-health-app-market-growth.svg`

## Optional Slide 6

### Title
Why now, and why us

### Body copy

- Youth are already using digital tools for emotional support
- AI is making personalization cheaper and more scalable
- Existing apps still leave a trust, usefulness, and emotional-fit gap
- `Mind Islands` is built specifically for self-compassion under chronic pressure

### Closing line
We are not trying to replace therapy. We are building the emotional operating layer that many young adults reach for before therapy, between therapy sessions, or instead of no support at all.
